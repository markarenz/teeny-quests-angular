How to Quest Your Best in Teeny Quest

In some levels, your goal is to collect as much loot as you can and find the special exit with a swirly pattern on the door. In others, you'll need to collect specific items or complete a task. The goal of the level is really up to the level designer.

"Designing levels?" you say?

Why, yes! Just register an account and go to the game editor screen to start crafting quests like a boss- or like an end boss if you're into puns. If not, just ignore that last bit.

The game saves after every action, so you can come back to any game and pick up where you left off. If you want to reset your progress, click the "reset progress" button on the game page.

Movement

Movement is very simple. Just click on the map cell where you want to move. You may notice that your movement is limited based on where you are and where you want to go. For example, if you're standing next to a big wall with an area on top to walk around, you won't see a green dot on that map cell to click on since there is no path from here to there. You'll need to explore and interact with your environment to find all the little surprises the level designers have laid out for you to find.

Low light can limit your movement as well. If you're in a dark area, your range of movement is reduced until you find a way to light up the area if one exists. If you light two torches, the entire area will be lit fully. Other items or props may emit light as well.

Exits

To use a door, stand next to it. If it's unlocked you can click on it to go through. If you find it locked, you'll need to get a key of the same color to get through, usually silver or gold. The exits don't have to be perfectly logical, so a designer may decide to make a door facing south open to the east because why not? Confusion is fun, and context is for boring people with no imagination.

Items

You found something. Fantastic! If it's an item you can take, stand in the same map cell and you'll see it in a bubble over your head where you can click on it to take it. Once you have an item in your inventory, you can drop it or use it. Using an item is contextual so the use button will only be available in certain circumstances. For example, if you're standing next to a door with a silver lock and you happen to have a silver key in your inventory, the use button will be enabled.

Interactive Props

There are all sorts of props and other elements in the world to interact with. If you see a switch on the wall click on it to turn something on or off. Maybe you turned on a torch or made a change in another room. Explore to find out. You can also click on banners, which change color when clicked and can act like switches with 3 states. Designers can use these as decorations or to service a puzzle.
